Davek na Promet Nepremicnin (Property Transfer Tax)

Slovenia's davek na promet nepremicnin (DPN) is a 2% property transfer tax on the sale of existing (used) real estate. New builds subject to VAT are exempt from DPN. The seller pays by default, though parties can agree otherwise.

Slovenia levies a 2% property transfer tax (davek na promet nepremicnin — DPN) on the transfer of used (second-hand) real estate. The tax base is the declared contract price or the market value, whichever is higher — FURS (the Slovenian Financial Administration) can challenge declarations it considers below market and issue an assessment.

By default, DPN is the seller's obligation. In practice, purchase contracts often shift the obligation to the buyer — confirm which party is contractually responsible before signing. Regardless of who pays, the tax must be settled with FURS before the land register will process the ownership change.

New properties sold by a developer and subject to VAT (DDV — davek na dodano vrednost) at 9.5% (residential) are exempt from DPN. This VAT exemption makes new-build purchases tax-equivalent to used property in many cases, though VAT and DPN operate on different bases.

The buyer's purchase costs include not only the 2% DPN but also land register fees (approximately EUR 50–150), notarial fees for the clausula intabulandi certification, and legal fees. Total transaction costs for a used Slovenian property typically run 3–5% of the purchase price.

DPN returns are filed with FURS within 15 days of the deed. Failure to file results in penalties. FURS can audit property transactions up to five years after the filing date, so accurate price reporting is essential.

Key Facts

Rate2% of contract price or market value (higher)

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming DPN is always the seller's problem is incorrect when your purchase contract assigns it to you. Read the odgovornost clause in every Slovenian purchase agreement carefully.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: File the DPN return with FURS promptly — the 15-day deadline runs from the deed date, not from the land register entry. Late filing penalties start from day 16.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if FURS disputes the declared price?

FURS can issue a revised assessment if the declared price appears below market. You have 30 days to appeal with supporting evidence (comparable sales, survey). If unsuccessful, the additional DPN must be paid.

Is DPN deductible from capital gains when I sell?

DPN paid on purchase forms part of your acquisition cost for capital gains tax purposes, reducing future taxable gain.

What is the VAT rate on new Slovenian residential property?

9.5% reduced rate applies to residential new builds. Commercial property and land attract the standard 22% VAT rate.
