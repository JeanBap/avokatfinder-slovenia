What is EMŠO (Enotna Matična Številka Občana)?

Slovenia's 13-digit unique citizen number (format DDMMYYY-RXXXK) — assigned at birth or first registration; required for employment, banking, healthcare, and property transactions.

Key Facts — EMŠO (Enotna Matična Številka Občana)
  • Format: DDMMYYY-RXXXK (13 digits encoding birth date, region, sequence, check)
  • Required for: employment, banking, healthcare, residency, property
  • EU residents: obtain at Upravna enota on residency registration — free
  • Non-resident property buyers: use Davčna številka (FURS) instead
  • Privacy protected — cannot be collected by private parties without statutory basis

The EMŠO — Enotna Matična Številka Občana — is Slovenia's 13-digit unique citizen identification number. Its format encodes the date of birth, the regional code, a sequential number, and a check digit: DDMMYYYRRXXXK. Every Slovenian citizen receives an EMŠO at birth; foreign nationals registering residency in Slovenia receive one upon registration with the Administrative Unit (Upravna enota). The EMŠO is Slovenia's equivalent of the Scandinavian personal number or the Czech rodné číslo.

The EMŠO is required for virtually all significant transactions in Slovenia: opening a bank account, entering an employment contract, registering with the Health Insurance Institute of Slovenia (ZZZS) for public healthcare, applying for residency documents, purchasing property, and enrolling children in state schools. It is printed on the Slovenian identity card (osebna izkaznica) and residence permit (dovoljenje za prebivanje).

EU/EEA nationals exercising free movement rights in Slovenia register their residency at the relevant Upravna enota (Administrative Unit) and receive an EMŠO as part of this process. The registration is free and requires a valid EU ID/passport, proof of Slovenian address (lease or property deed), and a statement of purpose (employment, study, or self-sufficiency). Non-EU nationals with a Slovenian residence permit also receive an EMŠO.

Foreign investors purchasing Slovenian property without establishing residency obtain a Davčna številka (tax number) from the Financial Administration (FURS) instead of an EMŠO. The Davčna številka is sufficient for the property purchase transaction. Your Slovenian lawyer or notary will advise on the correct identifier for your specific situation and can apply for the Davčna številka on your behalf.

EMŠO data is protected under Slovenian privacy law. The EMŠO cannot be freely collected by private parties for commercial purposes — only authorities and organisations with a specific statutory basis may request it. However, notaries, employers, and banks may legitimately collect it for their regulated activities. Unauthorised collection or misuse of EMŠO data is a violation of Slovenian personal data protection law.

Common Mistake: EU nationals who start working in Slovenia before completing residency registration try to use their EHIC for healthcare, not realising that Slovenian public health insurance (ZZZS) requires employment registration and an EMŠO to access anything beyond emergency treatment. Register your residency and EMŠO in the first week of arriving.
Expert Tip: If you register Slovenian residency while also owning or renting a property in your home country, Slovenia may consider you tax-resident in Slovenia if you spend more than 183 days per year there, regardless of your official registration. Consult a Slovenian tax adviser early to understand your Slovenian personal income tax obligations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get an EMŠO in Slovenia as an EU citizen?

Register your residency at the Upravna enota (Administrative Unit) for your address. Bring a valid EU ID or passport, proof of Slovenian address, and a statement of your purpose for staying (employment letter, university enrolment, or self-sufficiency declaration). The EMŠO is assigned as part of this registration and typically appears on your residence registration certificate.

Do I need an EMŠO or Davčna številka to buy property in Slovenia?

Non-resident buyers use a Davčna številka (tax number) from FURS rather than an EMŠO. Resident foreigners with an EMŠO use that. Your Slovenian notary (notar) will confirm which identifier is needed and can help you obtain the Davčna številka if required before the deed is signed.

What is the Davčna številka in Slovenia?

The Davčna številka is Slovenia's 8-digit tax identification number issued by the Financial Administration (FURS). For natural persons who are Slovenian residents, the Davčna številka is derived from the EMŠO. For non-residents, it is a separately issued number used for tax obligations including property ownership, rental income, and capital gains on Slovenian real estate.
