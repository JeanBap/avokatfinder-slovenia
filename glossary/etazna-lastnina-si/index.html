Etazna Lastnina (Condominium / Sectional Ownership)

Etazna lastnina is Slovenian sectional ownership — the legal framework for individually owned apartments within a multi-unit building. Each owner holds freehold title to their unit and a co-ownership share of the common parts.

Etazna lastnina (condominium ownership) is the Slovenian legal structure for multi-family buildings. Each apartment owner holds full freehold title (lastninska pravica) to their individual unit and simultaneously holds an undivided co-ownership share (solasten delez) in the common parts — the structure, stairwells, lifts, roof, and shared systems.

The co-ownership share in common parts is expressed as a fraction proportional to the unit's floor area relative to the total building area. This fraction determines the owner's voting weight in the soupravniki (co-owners' assembly) and their contribution to shared maintenance costs (stroskovnik).

Every etazna lastnina building is required by law to maintain a rezervni sklad (reserve fund) for future maintenance and repairs. Contributions are mandatory and are calculated as a percentage of the reconstructed value per square metre of the unit. The fund is administered by an upravljavec (building manager) appointed by the co-owners.

The upravljavec prepares an annual maintenance plan and budget, manages the building's common services, and ensures compliance with fire safety, lift inspection, and energy regulations. Major works (structural repairs, roof replacement, lift renewal) require a decision by the co-owners' assembly.

Buyers must request the building's rezervni sklad balance and the most recent upravljavec report before exchange. A depleted reserve fund combined with upcoming major works is a strong indicator that special contributions (izredni strosek) are imminent.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not checking the rezervni sklad balance before buying an etazna lastnina apartment can result in an immediate special levy for major works already in planning. This is one of the most common post-purchase financial surprises in Slovenian property.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask the upravljavec for the building's 10-year maintenance plan alongside the current reserve fund balance. Comparing planned works against available funds instantly reveals whether a special assessment is coming.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I renovate my apartment without the co-owners' consent?

Internal works that do not affect the structure or common services generally do not require consent. Facade changes, structural alterations, or works affecting shared utilities require a co-owners' assembly decision.

What is the izredni strosek?

An extraordinary contribution levied on all unit owners when the reserve fund is insufficient to cover a necessary repair. It is proportional to each owner's floor-area share and can be substantial for major works like roof replacement.

Can the assembly force me to contribute to a renovation I oppose?

Yes. Decisions made by a lawful majority of the co-owners' assembly are binding on all owners. Dissenting owners must still contribute their proportional share to approved works.
