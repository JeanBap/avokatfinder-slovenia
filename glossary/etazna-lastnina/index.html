Etažna Lastnina

Etažna lastnina is Slovenian condominium ownership — a form of property ownership combining exclusive title to an individual unit with a proportional share of common areas in a multi-unit building.

Etažna lastnina (floor ownership / strata title) is the standard ownership structure for apartments and units in Slovenian multi-storey buildings. Each owner holds exclusive title to their unit and an undivided proportional share in the building's common areas (skupni deli), including staircases, lifts, roof, and land.

The rights and obligations of etažna lastnina owners are governed by the Stvarnopravni zakonik (Property Code) and the Stanovanjski zakon (Housing Act). Owners form a condominium community (skupnost etažnih lastnikov) and are collectively responsible for maintaining common areas.

Etažna lastnina owners contribute to a reserve fund (rezervni sklad) for building maintenance. The minimum monthly contribution is set by regulation based on the building's age and area of the individual unit. Failure to contribute can result in enforcement action by the condominium manager (upravnik).

Before purchasing an etažna lastnina unit, buyers should request the condominium's reserve fund balance, the latest maintenance plan, and information on any outstanding special assessments. A well-funded reserve indicates responsible building management.

Common Mistake: Overlooking the reserve fund (rezervni sklad) when buying a Slovenian apartment. An underfunded reserve fund means future special assessments for major repairs will fall on owners — including new buyers who had no input into maintenance decisions.
Expert Tip: Request three years of condominium accounts and the current maintenance plan (načrt vzdrževanja) from the upravnik before signing. This reveals the building's maintenance history and upcoming capital expenditure requirements.

The upravnik (building manager) is appointed by the etažna lastnina community and is responsible for day-to-day building management, maintenance, and financial administration. Major decisions require a majority vote at a general meeting (zbor lastnikov).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a rezervni sklad in Slovenian condominium ownership?

A rezervni sklad is a mandatory reserve fund maintained by etažna lastnina communities for building maintenance and repairs. Monthly contributions are set by regulation based on building age and unit size. New buyers inherit any outstanding contribution obligations.

Who manages a Slovenian condominium building?

An upravnik (building manager) is appointed by the etažna lastnina community. The upravnik handles day-to-day management, maintenance, accounting, and legal compliance on behalf of all owners.

Can a non-EU citizen buy etažna lastnina in Slovenia?

EU citizens may purchase etažna lastnina in Slovenia on the same terms as Slovenian nationals. Non-EU citizens may purchase under bilateral agreements or with prior approval from the competent authority. Some restrictions apply to agricultural and forest land.
