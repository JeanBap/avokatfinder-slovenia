Gradbeno Dovoljenje (Building Permit)

A gradbeno dovoljenje is the Slovenian building permit required for construction, major renovation, or change of use of any structure. Buyers of existing properties should verify that all structures have valid permits.

In Slovenia, any new construction, major structural alteration, or change of use requires a gradbeno dovoljenje (building permit) from the local administrative unit (upravna enota). The permit is issued after the submitted plans comply with the spatial planning act (prostorski nacrt) and building code (gradbena zakonodaja).

From 2021, Slovenia introduced a reformed building permit system under the GZ-1 (Gradbeni Zakon 2021). Key changes include the introduction of a simplified permit category for minor works and a stricter timeline for the final inspection (tehnicni pregled) and use permit (uporabno dovoljenje) after construction completion.

The uporabno dovoljenje (use permit) is distinct from the gradbeno dovoljenje — the building permit authorises construction; the use permit certifies that the completed building may be legally occupied. Both are required for the property to be fully legal. A building with a gradbeno dovoljenje but no uporabno dovoljenje is structurally permitted but may not be legally inhabited.

Buyers should request both documents for every structure on a purchased property. Agricultural outbuildings, garages, and extensions are frequently constructed without permits in Slovenian rural areas. An unpermitted structure creates a risk that the owner may be ordered to demolish it.

Permit history can be checked through the e-uprava.gov.si portal using the plot's parcel number. The local upravna enota can also provide certified extracts of permit records for a specific property.

Common Mistake

Watch out: A property with a building permit but no use permit (uporabno dovoljenje) cannot be legally occupied. This is a common situation with older renovations and can create mortgage difficulties. Verify both permits, not just one.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For properties on agricultural land in Slovenia, check whether the land's spatial use classification (namenska raba) permits residential construction. Building permits on agriculturally classified land are heavily restricted.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the tehnicni pregled?

The technical inspection (tehnicni pregled) is carried out by an authorised inspector after construction is complete but before the uporabno dovoljenje is issued. It verifies that the building matches its approved plans and meets safety standards.

Can I use an unpermitted building?

Physically yes, but legally no. Unpermitted use of a building is an administrative offence. More practically, an unpermitted building cannot be insured, mortgaged, or legally sold without disclosure and risk of enforcement.

What happens if I buy a property with an unpermitted extension?

You inherit the risk. The local authority can order demolition or impose fines on the current owner. Regularisation (naknadna pridobitev dovoljenja) may be possible if the structure complies with current rules — check before buying.
