Hipoteka

Hipoteka is the Slovenian term for a mortgage or charge over real estate — a security interest registered in the Zemljiška Knjiga giving the lender rights over the property if the borrower defaults.

In Slovenia, a hipoteka is established by notarial deed and registered in the C sheet of the Zemljiška Knjiga. The registration gives the lender a real right (stvarna pravica) against the property, enforceable against any subsequent owner. Priority between competing hipoteke is determined by order of registration.

Slovenian banks require a hipoteka as security for any mortgage loan (stanovanjski kredit). The loan-to-value ratio is regulated by the Bank of Slovenia (Banka Slovenije) — typically up to 80% of appraised value for primary residences. The appraiser must be independent and certified.

When buying a Slovenian property with an existing hipoteka, the purchase contract should include a clause requiring the seller to discharge the mortgage from the purchase proceeds at completion. The buyer's lawyer should coordinate simultaneous repayment and deregistration to avoid taking on the encumbrance.

After repayment of a Slovenian mortgage, the bank issues a izbrisna pobotnica (discharge receipt). This document must be filed with the Zemljiška Knjiga by the borrower's lawyer or notary to formally remove the hipoteka from the C sheet.

Common Mistake: Completing purchase without confirming simultaneous discharge of the seller's hipoteka. In Slovenia, the hipoteka follows the property — not the person — so an undischarged lien binds the new owner even after the seller's loan is technically repaid.
Expert Tip: Structure the closing so that a portion of the purchase price equal to the outstanding hipoteka balance is paid directly to the lender, with simultaneous submission of the izbrisna pobotnica to the Zemljiška Knjiga before the balance is released to the seller.

Never complete a Slovenian property purchase without verifying that any existing hipoteka shown in list C is to be discharged at closing. An undischarged hipoteka passes with the property to the new owner.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an izbrisna pobotnica in Slovenia?

An izbrisna pobotnica is a discharge receipt issued by the Slovenian mortgage lender confirming the loan has been repaid. It is submitted to the Zemljiška Knjiga to remove the hipoteka from list C of the land register.

Can a Slovenian hipoteka be enforced against a new owner?

Yes. Since a hipoteka is a registered real right in the Zemljiška Knjiga, it follows the property regardless of ownership changes. A buyer who takes title without ensuring the hipoteka is discharged becomes bound by it.

What LTV ratio applies to Slovenian mortgage loans?

The Bank of Slovenia generally limits residential mortgage loans to 80% of appraised value for primary residences, with stricter limits for investment properties and buyers with existing mortgage commitments.
