Kupoprodajna Pogodba

A kupoprodajna pogodba is the Slovenian sale and purchase agreement — the binding written contract transferring ownership of real property from seller to buyer in Slovenia.

The kupoprodajna pogodba (purchase agreement) is the central instrument in any Slovenian property transaction. Slovenian law requires the contract to be in writing and the seller's signature must be notarially certified (notarsko overjena) for the transfer to be registerable with the Land Registry (Zemljiška Knjiga).

The contract must identify both parties with their EMŠO (personal identification number), provide the precise cadastral description of the property, state the agreed purchase price, outline payment terms, and include any conditions precedent. Buyers should ensure the contract also includes representations from the seller that the property is free of encumbrances.

Once signed, the contract is submitted to the Zemljiška Knjiga for registration of the ownership transfer (prenos lastninske pravice). Registration is constitutive in Slovenia — the buyer does not become the legal owner until the entry is made in the Land Registry.

Slovenian law imposes a general pre-emption right (predkupna pravica) in favour of co-owners and sometimes the municipality. Sellers must offer the property to any holders of predkupna pravica before selling to a third party, or risk the transaction being challenged.

Common Mistake: Assuming verbal agreements or informal written notes constitute a binding Slovenian property contract. Only a properly notarially certified kupoprodajna pogodba submitted to the Zemljiška Knjiga creates legal ownership.
Expert Tip: Request a certified extract from the Zemljiška Knjiga (zemljiškoknjižni izpisek) the day before signing to confirm the property's encumbrance status — liens can be registered quickly and an outdated extract may be misleading.

International buyers in Slovenia should engage a Slovenian notary or lawyer to verify the kupoprodajna pogodba and coordinate Land Registry submission, as errors in the cadastral description or missing certifications cause registration rejections.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is required for a valid Slovenian property purchase agreement?

A kupoprodajna pogodba must be in writing with the seller's signature notarially certified. It must include full party identification, precise cadastral property description, purchase price, and payment terms. Submission to the Zemljiška Knjiga completes the legal transfer.

What is the Slovenian real estate transfer tax?

Slovenia levies a 2% real estate transfer tax (davek na promet nepremičnin) on resale property transactions. New-build sales by VAT-registered developers are subject to 9.5% VAT instead. The tax is paid by the seller unless contractually agreed otherwise.

How long does Slovenian Land Registry registration take?

Registration in the Zemljiška Knjiga typically takes two to four weeks from submission of the correctly certified kupoprodajna pogodba. During this period, a plomba notation appears on the land register extract.
