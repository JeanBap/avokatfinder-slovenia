Notarski Zapis

A notarski zapis is a Slovenian notarial deed — a document prepared and authenticated by a Slovenian notary that carries full legal authority and is directly enforceable without further court proceedings.

The notarski zapis is the highest form of legal document in Slovenia. When a property contract is executed as a notarski zapis, it constitutes an executable title (izvršilni naslov), meaning that if either party defaults, enforcement can proceed directly without a court judgment.

For standard Slovenian property sales, only the seller's signature requires notarial certification (overitev podpisa) rather than a full notarski zapis. However, transactions involving mortgages, usufruct rights, or commercial properties typically require the full notarski zapis form for enforceability.

Slovenian notaries (notarji) are licensed by the Chamber of Notaries of Slovenia (Notarska Zbornica) and operate as independent public officials. Their fees are regulated by the Notarial Tariff Act and are calculated based on the value of the transaction.

A notarski zapis for a mortgage agreement means the lender can initiate enforcement proceedings directly against the property without first obtaining a court judgment — a significant advantage that reduces the cost and time of debt recovery.

Common Mistake: Confusing overitev podpisa (certified signature) with a full notarski zapis. The former authenticates the signature only; the latter creates a full notarial deed with direct executability. They are not equivalent in legal effect.
Expert Tip: For off-plan Slovenian property purchases, insist on a notarski zapis rather than just a certified contract. The direct enforceability provides crucial protection if the developer delays or defaults before construction completes.

For complex Slovenian property transactions — particularly those involving multiple owners, foreign buyers, off-plan purchases, or commercial elements — opting for a full notarski zapis rather than mere signature certification provides stronger legal protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is a notarski zapis required in Slovenia?

A notarski zapis is required for mortgage agreements (hipoteka), usufruct rights, and commercial property transactions. Standard residential resales require only notarial certification of the seller's signature rather than a full notarial deed.

How are Slovenian notarial fees calculated?

Fees are set by the Notarska Tarifa (Notarial Tariff) and are based on the transaction value. For a property purchase at €200,000, total notarial fees typically range from €400 to €900 depending on the services provided.

Is a notarski zapis publicly accessible in Slovenia?

Notarski zapisi are not publicly accessible as they are private legal documents. However, rights registered in the Zemljiška Knjiga based on notarski zapisi are publicly visible in the land register extract.
