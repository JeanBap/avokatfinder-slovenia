Odmera Davka (Tax Assessment for Property)

Odmera davka is the Slovenian tax authority's assessment process for property transfer tax and capital gains tax. All property transactions must be reported to FURS within 15 days, and FURS issues a formal assessment before registration proceeds.

When a Slovenian property changes hands, the parties must report the transaction to FURS (Financna uprava Republike Slovenije — Slovenian Financial Administration) within 15 days of the deed date. FURS then issues a tax assessment (odmera davka) for DPN (davek na promet nepremicnin — 2% transfer tax). The odmera is a formal administrative decision that specifies the tax due, payment deadline, and appeal rights.

FURS assesses DPN on the basis of the declared contract price. If FURS determines the declared price is below market value, it can issue an amended odmera based on its own valuation (tezisca trganja). The taxpayer has 30 days to appeal the assessment with supporting market evidence.

Capital gains on property are also assessed through the odmera process. Slovenian capital gains tax on property is levied at 25% on the profit from sale, reducing by 5% for each five-year holding period: after 5 years it is 15%; after 10 years, 10%; after 15 years, 5%; after 20 years, 0%. The holding period runs from the date of acquisition recorded in the land register.

Capital gains must be declared to FURS by the seller within 15 days of the notarial deed. The capital gain is calculated as the sale price minus the acquisition cost, adjusted for inflation (index rasti cen). Documented improvement costs incurred within the ownership period are deductible.

Failure to file within 15 days results in automatic penalty charges. FURS conducts post-transaction audits of property deals and discrepancies between declared and market prices attract investigation.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Declaring a transfer price below market value to reduce DPN is counterproductive — FURS's property valuation database covers most of Slovenia and the assessment will be corrected. The penalty interest on an amended assessment often exceeds the attempted saving.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For sellers approaching the 5, 10, 15, or 20-year capital gains threshold, timing the signing of the notarial deed to fall after the anniversary date can materially reduce the CGT rate. A few weeks' delay can save thousands of euros.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the 20-year CGT exemption clock start?

From the date the acquisition was registered in the zemljiska knjiga — not the date of the purchase contract. Always verify the exact registration date from the land register extract.

What costs can the seller deduct from the capital gain?

The original purchase price (acquisition cost) is the main deduction, adjusted for inflation using the official index. Documented improvement costs (proven by invoices) can also be deducted. Sale costs such as agent commission and legal fees are not deductible for Slovenian CGT purposes.

Can FURS audit a sale from five years ago?

Yes. FURS has a five-year statute of limitations for property tax assessments from the date of the filing deadline. Transactions that were misreported remain at risk of reassessment for five years.
