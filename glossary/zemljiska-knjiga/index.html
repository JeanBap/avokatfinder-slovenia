What is Zemljiška knjiga (Land Register)?

Slovenia's electronic land register recording property ownership, mortgages, and easements — publicly accessible online. Registration (vknjižba) is required for the transfer to be effective against third parties.

Key Facts — Zemljiška knjiga (Land Register)
  • Public search: eZemljiska knjiga portal at the Slovenian courts website
  • Ownership effective vs. third parties only on vknjižba (registration)
  • Plomba (pending note) entered immediately on filing — protects buyer's priority
  • Check: owner, mortgages (hipoteke), easements, pre-emption rights, pending proceedings
  • Notary submits registration application after signing — typically 10–30 days processing

The Zemljiška knjiga is Slovenia's official land register, maintained by district courts (okrajna sodišča) and accessible online through the eZemljiska knjiga portal at ezdrav.si/evknjiga. It records the ownership of every parcel of land and every building unit in Slovenia, along with all registered rights (easements, pre-emption rights, lifelong rights of use) and encumbrances (mortgages, liens, court orders). The register is publicly searchable and provides the authoritative legal record of property rights in Slovenia.

The vknjižba — cadastral/land register inscription — is the act of registering a new right in the Zemljiška knjiga. Ownership transfer takes full legal effect as against third parties only upon vknjižba, not upon signing the notarial deed. Until the registration is processed, the buyer has a valid contractual right but is not yet the publicly recognised owner. A pending note (plomba) is entered immediately on submission of the registration application, protecting the buyer's priority position against any later-filed applications.

Before completing any Slovenian property purchase, your lawyer or notary should check the Zemljiška knjiga extract (zemljiškoknjižni izpisek) for the target property. This shows: the registered owner (imetnik pravice); the exact cadastral area (parcela) and land use classification; any mortgages (hipoteke) or financial charges; any easements (stvarne služnosti) or personal rights (osebne služnosti) such as lifetime rights of use; any legal pre-emption rights; and any pending proceedings.

In Slovenia, all property is identified by its katastrska občina (cadastral municipality) and parcelna številka (parcel number). For apartment purchases, the property is registered as a posamezni del (individual unit) within a multi-unit building. The SPI (Sistem pravnih informacij — legal information system) of the Slovenian courts provides online access to land register data, though official certified extracts require a court fee application.

Slovenian land register registration is handled by the Slovenian notary system: after the notarial deed is executed, the notary submits the registration application (predlog za vknjižbo) to the relevant district court. Processing typically takes 10–30 days. The notarial system provides an efficient pipeline from deed signing to registration, with the notary handling all the administrative steps on behalf of the parties.

Common Mistake: Buyers who do not verify the Zemljiška knjiga before signing a preliminary agreement sometimes discover an existing lifetime right of use (osebna služnost) registered in favour of a third party — often an elderly previous owner. Such rights can significantly affect the property's value and practical use. Always check the full extract before paying any deposit.
Expert Tip: Request a certified Zemljiška knjiga extract (overjen izpisek) from the notary on the day of signing, not just a days-old printout. The notary has direct access to the real-time register and should confirm the register's current status immediately before executing the deed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I check property ownership in Slovenia?

Search the electronic Zemljiška knjiga through the Slovenian courts portal using the property's katastrska občina (cadastral municipality) and parcelna številka (parcel number), or through the e-sodstvo portal. The search is free and provides the current ownership, encumbrances, and registered rights. For certified extracts, apply to the relevant okrajno sodišče.

What is a hipoteka in Slovenian property law?

A hipoteka is a registered mortgage or charge over Slovenian real estate. It is registered in the Zemljiška knjiga in favour of the lender (typically a bank) as security for a loan. Before completing a purchase, any existing hipoteka must be discharged and removed from the register. Your lawyer should ensure the seller uses part of the purchase price to discharge any existing mortgages.

How long does land register registration take in Slovenia?

After the notary submits the registration application, the district court typically processes it within 10–30 days. Expedited processing is available in urgent circumstances. During the processing period, the plomba (pending note) protects the buyer's priority position against any subsequently filed applications.
